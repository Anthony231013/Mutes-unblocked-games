How to Draw a Person

A simple step‑by‑step guide for beginners.

Start Drawing

Before you start

You don’t need to be “good at art” to draw a person. You just need simple shapes, patience, and practice.tap here for a drawing website

  • Tools: Pencil, eraser, paper (or a drawing tablet).
  • Mindset: Focus on progress, not perfection.

Step‑by‑step guide

1. Start with a stick figure

Lightly sketch a stick figure to capture the pose: a circle for the head, a line for the spine, and lines for arms and legs.

2. Add simple 3D forms

Turn the stick figure into basic volumes: cylinders for arms and legs, a box or oval for the ribcage, and a smaller shape for the pelvis.

3. Block in the head and face

Draw an oval for the head. Add a vertical line down the middle and a horizontal line halfway for the eyes. Place the nose halfway between eyes and chin, and the mouth halfway between nose and chin.

4. Refine the body outline

Use the simple forms as a guide and draw the outer contour of the body—neck, shoulders, torso, hips, arms, and legs. Keep your lines light so you can adjust.

5. Add hands and feet

Start with simple shapes: a mitten shape for the hand and a wedge for the foot. Don’t worry about details yet—focus on proportion and direction.

6. Add clothing and details

Draw clothing over the body, following the underlying forms. Add hair, facial features, and any accessories that fit the character.

7. Clean up and shade

Erase construction lines you no longer need. Darken your final lines and add simple shading to show light and shadow.

Practice tips

  • Gesture drawing: Do 1–2 minute sketches to capture poses quickly.
  • Study references: Use photos or pose websites to learn real proportions.
  • Break it down: Practice heads, hands, and poses separately.
  • Draw daily: Even 10 minutes a day builds skill over time.
